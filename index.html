I am a Postdoctoral Associate in the Department of Mathematics and Statistics at Boston University. Prior to this, I was a PPFP postdoc in the Department of Mathematics at UCLA. I got my PhD at the University of Michigan in May 2024, advised by Sarah Koch, and my undergraduate degree at the Chennai Mathematical Institute in India.

My research interests lie in holomorphic dynamics in one variable, Teichmüller theory and mapping class groups. Lately I have also been studying algorithmic fairness in voting systems, in particular ranked choice voting and the Borda system.
