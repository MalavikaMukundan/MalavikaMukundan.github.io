I am a President's Postdoctoral Fellow in the Department of Mathematics at UCLA. I got my PhD at the University of Michigan in 2024, advised by Sarah Koch. Prior to this, I was an undergraduate at the Chennai Mathematical Institute in India.

Starting September 2024, I will be a Postdoctoral Associate in the Department of Mathematics and Statistics at Boston University.

My research interests lie in holomorphic dynamics in one variable, Teichmüller theory and mapping class groups. Lately I have also been studying algorithmic fairness in voting systems, in particular ranked choice voting and the Borda system.
